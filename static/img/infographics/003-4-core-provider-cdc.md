
## Goal

Professional infographic in English, executive stakeholder style, vertical format 1080x1600, clean modern enterprise presentation design. Large title: "Krateo Platform: Core Provider and CDC". Subtitle: "Who enables services and who runs them operationally". Color palette: teal blue, sage green, warm sand, and orange accents for risks. Light background with subtle geometric shapes and flow lines. Elegant, highly readable typography, strategic consulting / architecture slide look.

Structure with 4 visual sections and simple icons:

1) Top section: "Roles" with two side-by-side columns.
Left column with platform/gear icon: "Core Provider" and 4 short bullets:
- Publishes new service types on the platform
- Generates CRDs, validations, webhooks, and permissions
- Activates the dedicated dynamic controller
- Governs model versioning and lifecycle
Right column with operations/rocket icon: "CDC (Composition Dynamic Controller)" and 4 short bullets:
- Manages individual service instances
- Executes deploy, upgrade, and delete through Helm
- Keeps the environment aligned with the desired state
- Applies instance-specific RBAC for each composition

2) Middle section: "How They Work Together" as a horizontal 4-step flow diagram with soft arrows:
"1. A team defines a service" -> "2. Core Provider turns it into a platform capability" -> "3. A dedicated CDC is created" -> "4. The CDC manages each operational instance"
Add small labels under the steps: "standardization", "governance", "automation", "operations".

3) Next section: "Value for Business and Operations" with 4 compact cards:
- Reduces manual work
- Increases consistency and control
- Speeds up onboarding of new services
- Improves security and scalability
Use light icons and green check markers.

4) Final highlighted section: "Risks Without the Core Provider" with a soft orange/red background and warning icons. List 5 clear risks:
- Every new service would require manual configuration
- More errors and inconsistencies across environments
- Governance, validation, and versioning would be less controlled
- Greater dependency on specialized technical skills
- Slower onboarding and lower platform scalability

Small centered footer sentence: "In short: the Core Provider enables the service; the CDC runs it and keeps it operational."

Final look: very tidy, corporate, readable on a slide, no watermark, no device mockup, all text in English.

### Layout

Use a highly structured and symmetrical layout with an executive slide aesthetic. Vertical composition with generous margins and a clear grid system. Divide the page into 4 distinct horizontal bands with consistent spacing between sections.

Top section: two side-by-side cards of equal size and equal visual weight, with rounded corners and a soft shadow. Place a small icon in the upper-left area of each card and a clearly visible heading. Keep all bullets left-aligned with even spacing.

Middle section: a horizontal 4-step flow on a single row. Each step should sit inside a small box or pill shape with a visible number. Connect the steps with soft arrows or thin curved lines. Add a small label below each step.

Third section: a 2x2 grid of four compact value cards, all equal in size, with a small icon above or beside the text. Keep the grid highly aligned and visually balanced.

Bottom section: one wide highlighted horizontal panel spanning almost the full width of the page, with a soft light orange or pale red background. Inside, place the section heading and 5 readable bullets in a single clean column, each with a subtle warning icon.

Main title centered at the top of the page, subtitle directly below it in a slightly smaller size. Small understated footer centered at the bottom.

Maintain a strong visual hierarchy:
- very prominent main title
- medium section headings
- smaller compact bullet text

Use a premium strategic-presentation look:
- lots of white space
- precise alignment
- consistent minimal icons
- no intrusive decorative elements
- no advertising-poster style
- no comic style
